Using social media today became more easy than ever. A lot of options appear, and we always tend to select the bigger ones.
I also was on different plattforms over the past decade and also spend a lot of time there. But more and more I realized that somehow thats not it or at least not how I wanted it to be.
Ranging from not seeing the content that I wanted, up to the „I actually don‘t care“ with the addition of not feeling well anymore after spending a lot of time each day. Of course there is not *the solution*, but I‘ve tried some of them over the years. I just want to share some parts of my journey.

tl;dr I‘ve had an on and off with social media, and redefined it for myself and now I am mostly on fediverse plattforms as personal choice.

## Part 1: Realisation
Selfreflection was my personal factor to realize whats going on. I saw an unhealthy relationship and usage of social media on myself, which was just consuming content from other people, and towards the end mostly content creators and advertisements. This build up the fear of creating stuff on my own and also kills the mood and resulted in bad mood, being stressed and getting extra lazy.
The timelines I regularily scrolled through were filled with indefinite content and also started to show less and less content of people I‘ve actually „followed“.
I started to accept things I‘ve seen on the Internet as given and not even trying to proof anything. This also was reflected in stories I‘ve quoted and also in selfconfidence to know things that I‘ve didn‘t even try once.

## Part 2: Social Media Detox?
Often heard, often recommended and very often marked as very effective. I also tried it for a short period of time. And I can at least say: Its refreshing! I would also argue, that I would need to do an even more strict detox then I did, but only the short periods where I set some hurdles to reach it, it proofs very effective. Methods i‘ve tried and can to some extend recommend trying (No particular order or effectiveness - best tried in combinations):
- ‚Screentime‘ on Apple Devices,
- Disabling Notifications
- Removing the Apps from the Homescreen (Just reachable via search), 
- Turning off Siri recommendations for Apps (as it would just pop up in search as number one app),
- Grey Scale-Mode for your phone (Surprisingly effective),
- Make use of the ‚Hidden Folder‘ (Bonus Feature: This automatically hides notifications for these apps),
- Using the mobile browser version instead of reccomended app,
- Disabling Accounts (or even deleting the account)

### Quick FAQ:
#### Are these methods perfect?
Short Answer: No!

#### Will these Methods work for you?
It depends! Its mostly based on your own willingness to change!

## Part 3: Questioning Platform Choices
Next up I‘ve sat back and questioned the choices of platforms I want to use. This is of course highly dependend on your ethical standpoint.
For myself I‘ve decided early on to move away from Meta. So WhatsApp, Facebook, Instagram are gone! (Actually this was the most freeing feeling, as these tended to feel like they are the biggest burdon). Also moving away from Twitter and LinkedIn back then felt like a big step in the right direction.
This concentrated my usage to Reddit and YouTube, which as of writing this, are the only ‚big tech‘ platforms I am on regularily. But even just concentrating my usage to two platforms felt like a step forward and since content is 90% reposted on all platforms anyway.

## Part 4: Analysis & Categorization
The next big step forward was to check-in with myself to see what type of content I actually consume, why I am watching it and how I feel while and/or after consuming it.Categorizing content was a big relief on its own for me. This also brought up a lot of content I actually don‘t wanna watch in the future as it doesn‘t make me happy or hasn‘t any reason why I watch it. It felt great to gain some power back to decide what I actually wanna watch and what I am interested in. This makes all choices more intentional and therefore I am much more aware and satisfied with what I watch.

## Part 5: Falling back but intentionally
This all sounds like the story in the books of how someone could get happy with his social media. But there is a part which I didn‘t told you yet. With all these learnings over the years, I am gaining back control of the ‚if‘ and ‚how‘ I wanna do things. As you can see on the homepage, I‘ve a lot if Icons of platforms I am linking to. These where intentional choices ideas to get a more healthy relationship with social media. Most of these plattforms are decentralized and the next gen of social media, as they feel like the way social media should be like. The most underated feature on the fediverse alternatives are chronological Feeds. This is big a relief, and also limits the time automatically of things you see on there, as you will reach the point where you see posts again and stop scrolling. And even reloading will not completely fuck with your timeline and just show them the same order as before.

## Part 6: Conclusion
All in all, I am happy now and can relax more while consuming (hopefully) less and try to create more stuff in different ways. I still have the urge to share some things online, but this is a more controlled and intentional channel and not driven by interests of others. Maybe you will see more content here (or on the linked platforms) in the future.
